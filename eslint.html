  1. February 14th, 2022 (1)

    • [[eslint]]'s `no-negated-condition`
      • goes hand in hand w/ people doing `{condition && <Foo />}` instead of `{!condition ? null : <Foo />}` in [[react]]
        • because e.g. when checking array's length with `&&` (`{array?.length && <Foo />}`), if array is defined, but it's length is 0 (i.e., array is `[]`), then you'll get a `0` rendered in the page.
        • and in general, handling both (all) conditions is better than handling some & not considering others.
